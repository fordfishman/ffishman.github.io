Welcome

Hi, I'm Ford Fishman. Currently, I am a graduate student at Indiana University in the Lennon Lab. I study the ecology and evolution of bacteria through a computational lens. My work uses simulation techniques, such as agent-based models, to how the interactions among microbes change and evolve over time. I've also developed methods to study how the evolutionary rates of bacteria and archaea might constrain global microbial diversity.

I am presently looking to move into a data science career. I love solving computational problems, and my work during my time in graduate school has taught me to be a better problem solver. I look forward to delving into data and using that information to make statistical inferences and predict future outcomes using machine learning algorithms.

I'm also interested in basketball analytics, particularly focused on the NBA. I'm interested in how to best project the development of NBA prospects that are in league or have yet to be drafted. Currently, I am interested in using machine learning approaches to project which prospects have the highest chances of becoming elite three point shooters. I am also working to create metrics to better quantify the collective 3pt shooting different lineups possess and seeing how predictive these metrics are of team success in the regular season and post-season. Stay tuned for blog posts!
