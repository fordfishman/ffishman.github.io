Research Interests

Microbial Community Interactions

Marker Genes in Microbial Evolution and Ecology

Measuring microbial diversity through genomics is difficult due to the sheer number of
individuals and species in a given environment, as well as genetic heterogeneity. To
study the ecology of microbial communities, it is common practice to use marker genes,
such as sections of the 16S rRNA gene, as a sort of proxy of actual diversity.
